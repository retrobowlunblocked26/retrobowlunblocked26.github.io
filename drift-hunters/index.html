Pro Tips for Mastering Drift Hunters

🔥 Keep the RPMs High

Drifting requires power. If you are in Automatic mode and the car shifts up too early, it will lose power and grip up. Use Manual mode and stay in 3rd gear.

💨 Tap the Brakes

If you are sliding too wide and about to hit a wall, do not pull the handbrake. Gently tap the regular brake (S) to shift the weight forward and tighten your turn.

🔧 Upgrade the Turbo

The best 'bang for your buck' performance upgrade in the garage is the Turbo. It dramatically increases your horsepower, making it much easier to sustain long drifts.

🎨 Adjust the Offset

In the Tuning menu, pushing your wheel offset outward (making the wheels stick out further) gives the car a wider footprint, making it much more stable during high-speed transitions.
